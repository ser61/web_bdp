General Price List

The Federal Trade Commission regulates funeral homes and has established a series of rules called The Funeral Rule. The Funeral Rule protects consumers’ right to purchase funeral goods and services separately.

The Funeral Rule requires every funeral home to have a General Price List. It is a written, itemized price list of all the items and services the funeral home offers. To help you sort out the peculiarities of funeral arrangement and General Price List, we have developed this calculator.

  1. Basic Services of the Funeral Director: $2,000.00

    A basic service charge includes, but is not limited to, staff to respond to initial request for service; arrangement conference with family or responsible party; preparation and filing of necessary certificates.

  2. Embalming: $800.00

    Except certain special cases, embalming is not required by law. It may be necessary, however, if you select certain funeral arrangements, such as a funeral with viewing.

  3. Other Preparations of the Body

    We can offer you additional services in case you or the relatives of the deceased request cremation or embalming. The range of such services includes hairstyling, autopsy, dressing, casketing etc.

  4. Transfer of Remains to Funeral Home: $325.00

    This is the typical set fee within a designated radius of the funeral home (i.e. 30 mile radius) but an additional fee per mile ($4.00 per loaded mile) outside of the designated radius may be assessed.

  5. Funeral Rituals

    At Memorise, you can order any kind of generally accepted funeral rituals, from funeral ceremonies and visitations to identification viewing and memorial services as a necessary part of any funeral.

  6. Funeral Home Facilities

    We provide our clients and their families with comfortable and welcoming environment, meeting their requests for organizing and catering funerals. There are a lot of amenities for your convenience.

  7. Automotive Equipment

    Our vehicles can be expected to be clean and in proper working condition at all times. Our drivers are versed on the best routes providing directions to family and friends that have to be present at the funeral.

  8. Immediate Burial:

    With an immediate burial the body is taken directly from the place of death (or from the morgue) to the cemetery. The body only stops at the funeral home to secure the necessary burial documentation.

  9. Remembrance or Memorial Package

    Memorise sets the standard for the best funeral stationery in the industry. Our goal is to provide families with the highest quality, and the most meaningful remembrance of their loved one that is possible.

Cremation

Caskets are not required for cremation unless one is desired by the family or there is to be a public viewing before the cremation. All that is required by OSHA is the OSHA approved minimum cardboard container and pouch which are cremated with the body. Other OSHA approved cremation containers are available for purchase as well. The basic containment materials are provided by our funeral home and are included in our direct cremation package.

We do not require embalming unless there is to be a public viewing with an open casket or in rare cases where the body is to be shipped long distances (usually internationally), before cremation. We invite immediate family members to privately view the deceased prior to cremation at no charge.

Funerals

A formal or informal ceremony or ritual prior to burial, a funeral service often provides a sense of closure to family and friends. Although your faith or culture may dictate some elements of a funeral service, you may want to personalize other elements of the service. For example, you may choose to open or close the casket.

Regardless of whether you or your loved one have opted for burial or for cremation, the funeral or memorial service fills an important role. It can:

  • Honor, recognize and celebrate the life of the deceased
  • Allow friends and family to say their last good-byes
  • Provide closure after the loss of a loved one
  • Allow friends to console the family of the loved one

A death is not the extinguishing of a light, but the putting out of the lamp because the dawn has come.

What Is A Green Funeral?

The essence of a green funeral is age-old elemental simplicity. It rejects the so-called traditional funeral with its stuffy, urban look, in favor of an outdoorsy, homespun, back-to-nature look. It prefers an unspoilt landscape to that of a conventional cemetery. As an important element of environmental concern, eco funeral:

  • Rejects cremation
  • Opts for burial in a site serving a conservation purpose
  • Creates an environment which is not visually definable as a burial ground
  • Reviles embalming
  • Requires a coffin or shroud locally made from natural materials
  • Forbids demarcation of the grave
